GUIDELINES  
1. Don’t Try to Build Everything 
> This is the biggest hackathon killer.
> Your problem statement is massive.
You CANNOT perfectly solve:
•Heat mapping
•Climate simulation
•AI prediction
•City planning
•Cost optimization
•Real-time satellite integration
in limited time.
Instead:
 > **Build ONE strong core 
Recommended winning core:
“AI-driven hotspot detection + optimized cooling recommendation” 
Then add small premium features.
**This wins more than 20 unﬁnished features. 
2. Your Biggest Weapon = Storytelling 
Judges remember stories.
Your pitch should feel like:
“Pune is becoming hotter yearly. Authorities struggle to identify high-risk heat 
zones and decide the best cooling intervention. Team Orion developed an 
explainable geospatial AI system that identiﬁes hotspots and recommends 
optimized cooling strategies using satellite and meteorological data.” 
That sounds premium.
Never say:
	 “We trained a random forest model.”
No judge cares initially.
First tell impact. > Then tech. 
3. Choose One City and Dominate It 
Huge advice.
DO NOT: 
❌ Train on all India
DO: 
✅ One city deeply
Example:
 > **Pune 
Why?
•Data easier
•Faster experimentation
•Local relevance
•Easier validation
You can say:
“Framework is scalable to other Indian cities.” 
**Judges will accept. 
4. Documentation = Secret Weapon 
>> Most teams ignore documentation.
You should create:
Research Bible (Master Doc) 
Contains:
•Problem statement breakdown
•Papers
•Dataset links
•Model choices
•Experiments
•Failures
•Results
•Screenshots
•Meeting notes
Why? 
	 >> Because during PPT:
Someone forgets? —— No issue. 
Everything documented.
5. DAILY Internal Review 
Every 3 days ask:
1.What is done? 
2.What is blocked? 
3.What failed? 
4.What changed? 
This prevents chaos.

•Results

•Screenshots

10. Prepare Answers for Judge Questions 
You WILL be grilled.
Prepare beforehand.
1. Why Random Forest? 
	 > Because interpretable and robust for tabular geospatial data.
2. Why not Deep Learning? 
	 > Insu ﬃcient labeled spatial thermal data + interpretability concerns.
3. Why these datasets? 
	 > Satellite + meteorological complement each other.
4. Limitations? 
	  > Cloud cover, temporal variation, city-speci ﬁc training.
**Judges LOVE honesty.
